A beach is a landform alongside a body of water which consists of loose particles. The particles composing a beach are typically made from rock or biological sources. Sediments settle in different densities and structures, depending on the local wave action and weather, creating different textures, colors and gradients or layers of material

Wild beaches, also known as undeveloped or undiscovered beaches, are not developed for tourism or recreation. Preserved beaches and their associated dune are important for protection from extreme weather for inland ecosystems and human infrastructure.
Some of the famous beaches in India are as follows:

Radhanagar Beach, Andaman

The Radhanagar Beach on the Havelock Island is the most popular tourist attraction of Andaman and Nicobar Island. It has been bestowed with the precious "Asia's best beach" award by Time Magazine

Agonda Beach, Goa

Agonda Beach is an isolated stretch of sea line located on the outskirts of Goa that helps you to relax amid natural wonders like soft warm sand and turquoise blue water

Dhanushkodi Beach, Tamil Nadu

Dhanushkodi Beach is the closest point from Sri Lanka and it is also believed to be the same place where Lord Rama along with Lord Hanuman and his army built the famous 'Setu' bridge to reach Lanka
